Steps To Getting My First Developer Job

  • Learn Html/Css
  • Learn Javascript
  • Research
  • Interview Prep
  • Job Search
Learn Html/Css
  1. Headfirst Html/Css Workbook
  2. Build First Project
  3. Freecodecamp Training
  4. Build 5 Freecodecamp Projects
  5. Review Work and Master Technique

This section should take about 2-3 months. After going through my book, training, and projects I will begin to learn the fine details and tricks for top of the line web-development.

After I complete a couple of projects, I will create a certain pattern or workflow to be able to complete my projects efficiently.



Learn Javascript
  1. Headfirst Javascript Workbook
  2. Javascript Youtube Courses
  3. Freecodecamp Training
  4. Build Freecodecamp Projects
  5. Build My Own Projects and Master Technique

This section should take 2-3 months. After going through and learning from all of my resources I will begin to contruct new projects, and improve my projects from the first section.

I want to make sure that for this section my projects have variety in function, since javscript has a greater variety of capabilities.



Research
  1. Research Frameworks
  2. Research JQuerey
  3. Research Databases
  4. Research VS Code User Knowledge
  5. Research Creating Responsive Layouts
  6. Apply Knowledge From Research

This section will be less structured than the rest. After looking at a good amount of job postings and writing down the skill requirements, I will begin to explore, research, and apply said skills. This can be done in practice, new projects, or adding on to old projects.

Some of the required skills listed on job postings will only require entry level knowledge so I will need to sort out which ones will require the most attention.



Interview Prep
  1. Read Interview Prep Book
  2. Practice Interview Questions
  3. Work On Resume
  4. Research People's Interview Experiences
  5. Get Github In Order

This section should take 1-2 months. In order to complete these steps I will have to make use of different opinions and experiences. This will require me to gather information from a variety of sources. The third step will be the most important because it will be my first impression to the companies I'm applying to.

It is very important that I go through all of my Github content and make sure there or no errors or typos and that it is my best work.

Practice as many interivew questions as possible, just incase they throw you a curveball.



Job Search
  1. Search In The Most Efficient Way Possible
  2. Search On Multiple Platforms
  3. Apply To At Least 5 Jobs A Day
  4. Record Any Required Skills Mentioned In The Job Listing
  5. Read Up And Practice Required Skills

Finally, The last section! This could end up being the longest or the shortest section depending on when a company takes me in. It is important that during this process I am reviewing all that I've learned and planning for my next phase of learning.

I MUST stick to the rule of applying at least 5 times a day and keep a positive attitude. I must also plan for the situation where I have to relocate in order to work.
